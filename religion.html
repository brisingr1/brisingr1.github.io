Living World Religions
Religion Points of Contact Points of Contrast
Judaism 1) Commitment to Scripture and covenant keeping
2) Synagogue services and Christian Assemblies
3) Goal of "repairing the world" 		1) Different expectations about the nature and mission of the Messiah
2) Christ invites us into a new kind of covenant
3) Prophets' promise of the indwelling Holy Spirit is fulfilled in Christ
Islam 1) Belief in one God
2) Belief in Divine Revelation
3) Importance of submission to God 		1) Different understandings of the attributes of God
2) Conviction about divinity of Jesus and his incarnation, crucifixion and resurrection
3) Nature of the Bible vs nature of the Qur'an
Hinduism 1) All-pervasiveness of the Divine
2) Spiritual vs material focus
3) Principle of reaping and sowing 		1) Understanding of the Divine as a personal vs impersonal "force"
2) Use of images to connect with the Divine
3) Grace vs Karma
Buddhism 1) Concern over the problem of suffering
2) The "impermanence" of all things
3) The value of meditation 		1) The Christian response to suffering as redemptive
2) Grace vs Karma
3) Historical foundation of Christian faith
Jainism 1) Commitment to nonviolence
2) Humility with regard to knowledge: "we see in a glass dimly"
3) Spiritual vs material focus 		1) Belief in Creator God who made us in his image
2) Knowing Christ and him crucified
3) Embracing God's good gifts vs extrememe asceticism
Sikhism 1) Belief in one God
2) Commitment to virtuous living
3) Importance of hospitality 		1) God reveals himself in Scripture and in Christ
2) Christian view of Scripture vs Sikh view of Guru Granth Sahib
3) Christian commitment to evangelism, inviting others into the kingdom
Baha'i 1) Belief in one God
2) Commitment to diversity and inclusion
3) Emphasis on a reasoned faith, not simply an inherited faith 		1) Jesus claims to be "the One and Only"
2) Idea of "progressive revelation" conflicts with Christian understanding revelation
3) Baha'i way of dealing with religious differences takes exception to the way adherents of various religion faiths interpret their own scriptures
Traditional Religion 1) Belief in Creator God
2) Holistic worldview
3) Positive relationship between humans and their environment 		1) Creator God has revealed himself more directly in Christ
2) Christian acknowledge spiritual forces of darkness, but do not seek their help through divination, mediums or any other means
3) Christ teaches us to pray for our daily bread, but we resist any fixation on this-worldly concerns
Religion in China 1) The concept of "the tao" translated "the Way (and "the Word" in Chinese versions of John 1:1)
2) Family loyalty and group identity that counters Western individualism
3) The power and place of tradition 		1) Decades of official atheism that make belief difficult for many
2) Syncretism is common and accepted
3) Pragmatic emphasis on "reciprocal relationships" does not square with Christ-like selflessness
